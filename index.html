Iran opened fire on the Gulf states. The war is no longer contained.

On the seventh day of the war, Iran widened its campaign beyond Israel and US forces to strike Saudi Arabia, Kuwait, Qatar, Bahrain, and the UAE. Three ballistic missiles were intercepted before reaching Prince Sultan Air Base in Saudi Arabia. Drones and missiles hit a southern Kuwaiti air base, setting it ablaze. Qatar intercepted 13 missiles, with one landing in its national waters. A hotel and two residential buildings were struck in Bahrain. The IRGC also claimed drone strikes on tankers in the Strait of Hormuz, including the Honduras-flagged Athe Nova, while a strike on the port of Duqm in Oman injured one worker.

The Strait of Hormuz is now effectively closed. An IRGC official confirmed the blockade earlier this week and threatened to set fire to any ship that passes through. Tanker traffic through the waterway, which carries roughly a quarter of the world's seaborne oil trade and a fifth of its LNG supply, has come to a near standstill. QatarEnergy halted activity at the world's largest LNG export facility after an Iranian drone attack. Oil futures are surging, with Brent crude pushing well past $85 a barrel. The Dow fell 784 points Thursday, wiping out its 2026 gains. Futures are down again this morning ahead of the February jobs report.

The US is simultaneously trying to engineer regime change from inside. The CIA is working to arm Kurdish forces in northern Iran, aiming to stretch Tehran's military and create a buffer zone for Israel. Trump called Kurdish leaders in Iraq and Iran this week, though the White House denied reports of any formal agreement. Trump also told reporters he must be involved in selecting Iran's next supreme leader, dismissing the likely successor Mojtaba Khamenei as a "lightweight." More than 1,200 people have been killed across Iran. Israel says it has conducted over 2,500 strikes with more than 6,000 weapons and is entering a "next phase."
