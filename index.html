Israel is bombing Tehran. Trump wants unconditional surrender. The economy is losing jobs.

Israel launched what it called a "broad-scale wave of strikes" on Tehran on the seventh day of the war, hitting Jomhuri Avenue, one of the capital's major thoroughfares. The Israeli military claims near-complete air superiority after destroying 80 percent of Iran's air defense systems across 2,600 targets using more than 6,000 munitions. Trump demanded "unconditional surrender." Iran's foreign minister told NBC that Tehran has not asked for a ceasefire and will not negotiate. More than 1,230 people have been killed across Iran. In Lebanon, Israeli strikes have killed at least 123 people in a parallel assault on Hezbollah from Tripoli to the southern border.

The war reached the Gulf states overnight. Iran fired missiles and drones at Saudi Arabia, Kuwait, Qatar, Bahrain, and the UAE. Three ballistic missiles were intercepted before reaching Prince Sultan Air Base. A Kuwaiti air base was set ablaze. Qatar intercepted 13 missiles. A hotel and two residential buildings were hit in Bahrain. The IRGC has effectively closed the Strait of Hormuz, with tanker traffic dropping to near zero. The waterway carries a quarter of the world's seaborne oil and a fifth of its LNG. QatarEnergy shut down the world's largest LNG export facility. European natural gas prices nearly doubled from the prior week. Brent crude is well past $85.

Then the jobs report landed. The US economy lost 92,000 jobs in February, far worse than the 50,000 gain economists expected. Unemployment rose to 4.4 percent. Health care shed 28,000 jobs, driven by a Kaiser Permanente strike. Manufacturing lost 12,000 despite tariffs aimed at reshoring. Federal employment fell by 10,000. It was the third month of job losses in the past five. The Dow had already fallen 784 points Thursday; futures dropped further Friday morning. The economic picture is now a war economy layered on top of a weakening labor market.
