Congress surrendered the war power. The president claimed the peace power too.

Both chambers of Congress voted this week to let the war with Iran continue without authorization. The House rejected a war powers resolution Thursday 212 to 219, one day after the Senate killed its version 47 to 53. Four Democrats crossed to vote no in the House; two Republicans crossed to vote yes. The constitutional question of who starts wars is now settled in practice, if not in law: the executive does, and the legislature watches.

Hours after the House vote, President Trump told Axios he must be directly involved in choosing Iran's next supreme leader, comparing the situation to Venezuela, where US strikes removed Nicolas Maduro. Trump called the likely successor, Mojtaba Khamenei, the late ayatollah's son, \"unacceptable.\" Under Iran's constitution, the supreme leader is chosen by the 88-member Assembly of Experts. Israel bombed the building in Qom housing that body on Tuesday.

The war itself continued to widen. A US Navy submarine torpedoed and sank the Iranian frigate IRIS Dena in international waters off Sri Lanka on Tuesday, killing at least 87 sailors. It was the first American submarine torpedo attack since World War II. Sri Lanka recovered the bodies; 61 crew remain missing. The UAE said it intercepted six ballistic missiles and 125 drones on Thursday, with debris injuring six people in Abu Dhabi. Israel's military chief said the campaign is entering its \"next phase\" after 2,500 strikes using more than 6,000 weapons. At least 1,230 people have been killed across Iran. Brent crude settled at $85.41, the highest since July 2024.

China set its GDP growth target at 4.5 to 5 percent, the lowest band in decades, acknowledging the structural slowdown from its property collapse and four consecutive years of deflation.
