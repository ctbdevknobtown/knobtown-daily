Iran installed a dynasty. The war has no off-ramp.

The Assembly of Experts on Sunday named Mojtaba Khamenei, the 56-year-old son of Ali Khamenei, as Iran's new supreme leader. The elder Khamenei was killed in the opening US-Israeli strikes on Tehran on February 28. The IRGC pressured the assembly into an accelerated vote: members who argued against the appointment were given limited speaking time before discussion was cut off. US and Israeli bombs hit the assembly's offices in Qom during the initial session on March 3, before the count was completed, forcing a second round two days later. Mojtaba Khamenei is a regime insider with deep IRGC ties, no governing experience, and no public following. His father had blocked his candidacy during his own lifetime.

The appointment closes the most visible path to a negotiated end to the conflict. Trump immediately rejected the new leader as "a lightweight" and "unacceptable," telling Axios he would need to approve any new Iranian leadership himself. His demand for Iran's "unconditional surrender," posted Friday, remains the only stated US condition for ending hostilities. Iran's government has rejected talks. There is no diplomatic channel, no ceasefire framework, and no mediator with standing on both sides.

The war is nine days old. Oil posted its biggest weekly gain in the history of crude futures trading, closing Friday above $90 a barrel with futures pressing toward $100. The Strait of Hormuz remains effectively closed. CENTCOM says it has struck more than 3,000 targets and destroyed 43 Iranian warships since February 28. At least 1,332 people have been killed across Iran, including 181 children. More than 200 are dead in Lebanon. Seven US servicemembers have died. Iranian missile launches are down 90 percent from day one. Iran is still fighting.
