The United States is at war with Iran, and nobody voted for it

American and Israeli forces struck targets across Iran for a fifth consecutive day Wednesday, extending an air campaign that began hours after Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei was killed March 1 in what US officials described as an Israeli intelligence operation. The Pentagon confirmed six American soldiers have been killed in a rocket attack on a logistics base in Kuwait, the first US combat deaths of the campaign.

The strikes have concentrated on Iran's nuclear infrastructure, air defenses, and Revolutionary Guard command centers. Tehran's ability to respond has been limited but not eliminated. Houthi and Hezbollah-affiliated groups launched retaliatory strikes against US assets in Iraq and the Red Sea, none causing significant damage so far.

Congress has not authorized the use of force. The White House cited Article II commander-in-chief authority and the 2001 AUMF, a legal theory several constitutional scholars have called untenable for a campaign against a sovereign state. A bipartisan group of senators introduced a war powers resolution Wednesday morning demanding a vote within 48 hours.
