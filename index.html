The United States is at war with Iran, and nobody voted for it

American and Israeli forces struck targets across Iran for a sixth day Thursday, extending an air campaign that began February 28 when strikes killed Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei in what US officials described as a joint Israeli-American operation. Six American soldiers were killed by an Iranian drone strike on a logistics center at Port Shuaiba in Kuwait on Sunday, the first US combat deaths of the campaign. All six were Army reservists.

The strikes have hit over 1,600 targets across Iran, including nuclear facilities at Natanz and Isfahan, air defenses, and Revolutionary Guard command centers. Iran has launched more than 500 ballistic missiles and 2,000 drones in retaliation, striking US bases across nine countries and closing the Strait of Hormuz. Brent crude has risen past $80. The conflict has spread to Lebanon, where Israel has intensified bombardment.

Congress has not authorized the use of force. The Senate voted 47-53 on Tuesday to reject a war powers resolution introduced by Senator Tim Kaine that would have required the president to seek congressional authorization. Senator Rand Paul was the only Republican to vote yes. Senator John Fetterman was the only Democrat to vote no. The House votes Thursday.
