Trump declared the war "very complete" and called Putin to discuss ending it.

Four days after demanding Iran's unconditional surrender, Trump told reporters the war is "very complete, pretty much" and that the United States is "very far ahead of schedule." He said Iran has "no navy, no communications, no Air Force" and that its drone manufacturing capacity has been destroyed. Separately, Trump spoke by phone with Vladimir Putin, who shared proposals for ending the conflict quickly, according to the Kremlin. The call is the first confirmed diplomatic contact between a major power and the United States about a ceasefire since the war began eleven days ago.

An eighth American servicemember has died. Iran's foreign minister told NBC News his country would not agree to a ceasefire while attacks continued. Whether Trump's call with Putin produces anything real or was simply a pressure release valve for the markets is the question that will define the next 48 hours.
