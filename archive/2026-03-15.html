Sunday, March 15, 2026

Trump said there's nothing left to bomb. Iran attacked three cargo ships and threatened every US-linked bank in the Gulf.

Two weeks into the war, the United States and Iran are describing two different conflicts. Trump told reporters Thursday that there is "practically nothing left" for the US military to bomb, that Iran has "no navy, no air force, no anti-aircraft apparatus at all," and that the war could end any time he wants. Israeli Defense Minister Israel Katz said the opposite: that operations would continue "without any time limit, as long as required, until we accomplish all objectives." Trump acknowledged the gap, telling The Times of Israel that ending the war would be a "mutual decision" with Netanyahu. That means Israel has a veto on when the United States stops fighting.

Iran, meanwhile, went after commercial shipping. Three cargo ships were struck by projectiles near the Strait of Hormuz on Thursday: a Thai-flagged bulk carrier, the Mayuree Naree, was hit and caught fire (three crew members are still missing); a Japanese container ship, the One Majesty, was damaged while anchored in the Persian Gulf; and a Marshall Islands-flagged carrier, the Star Gwyneth, took a hull hit northwest of Dubai. Two drones fell near Dubai International Airport, injuring four people. Tehran warned it would not allow "even a single liter of oil" through the strait and said it would begin targeting US-linked banks across the Middle East. The war has moved from military infrastructure to the commercial arteries of the global economy.

The IEA's 32 member nations unanimously approved the 400-million-barrel reserve release on Wednesday, the largest in the agency's history. Oil held near $88 a barrel, down from $119 at Monday's peak but still far above pre-war levels. The reserves buy roughly two months. The ship attacks suggest Iran intends to make sure the strait stays closed well beyond that. The Pentagon is investigating how a US strike hit a girls' school in Minab early in the war, killing more than 160 people. More than 1,300 are dead in Iran, 500 in Lebanon, 12 in Israel. Nine Americans have died. The president says there's nothing left. The ships are burning.
