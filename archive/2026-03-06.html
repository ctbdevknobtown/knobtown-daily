Friday, March 6, 2026

Oil past $90. Iran running out of missiles. Goldman says stagflation.

US crude broke $90 a barrel on Friday for the first time since 2022, up 35 percent in a single week. That is the largest weekly gain since oil futures trading began in 1983. The Strait of Hormuz remains closed. Gas prices jumped another 7 cents overnight to $3.32 a gallon and are accelerating. Goldman Sachs chief economist Jan Hatzius said the US is entering a period of stagflation: rising prices driven by the energy shock layered on top of a labor market that just posted its worst month since the pandemic. The Dow closed down 453 points. The S&P 500 fell 1.33 percent. The Nasdaq dropped 1.59 percent.

Iran's ability to fight back is collapsing. The Pentagon said Iranian ballistic missile launches have fallen 90 percent since day one of the conflict, and drone attacks are down 83 percent. Israel struck 10 high-rise buildings in southern Beirut overnight and launched another wave of strikes on Tehran. At least 1,332 people have been killed across Iran, including 181 children according to UNICEF. More than 95,000 people have been displaced in Lebanon. Britain, France, and Spain have agreed to provide military support to protect allied interests in the region. British counter-drone helicopters began arriving in Cyprus on Friday.

The February jobs report got worse on a second look. December was revised from 50,000 jobs added to a contraction of 17,000, meaning three of the last five months have now been net negative. January was revised down from 130,000 to 126,000. The headline number, a loss of 92,000 jobs in February, is the worst single month since the pandemic lockdowns. Unemployment is 4.4 percent. The combination of a hot war disrupting a quarter of the world's oil supply and a cooling labor market is the textbook definition of stagflation. Markets are pricing in rate cuts, but the Fed's hands may be tied by energy-driven inflation.
