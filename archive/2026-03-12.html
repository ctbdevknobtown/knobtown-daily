Thursday, March 12, 2026

The Pentagon launched its biggest bombing day yet. Oil fell 7 percent because Trump said the war was ending.

Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth stood at a Pentagon podium Tuesday and announced that the day would bring the most intense US strikes on Iran since the war began. The most fighters, the most bombers, the most targets. Joint Chiefs Chairman Gen. Dan Caine said the US and Israel have now struck more than 5,000 targets and achieved a 90 percent reduction in Iran's missile launch capacity. At the same time, Brent crude fell more than 7 percent to around $92 a barrel, its steepest single-day drop since the war started, because Trump told reporters the conflict would be over "very soon." The markets chose to believe the president. The Pentagon chose to escalate.

The contradiction is not accidental. Trump's rhetoric is doing real work: the oil crash from Monday's $119 peak saved the global economy from the worst of the immediate shock, and Wall Street staged a recovery on ceasefire hopes. But the military campaign tells a different story. Iran launched fresh barrages at Saudi Arabia, Kuwait, and Israel on Tuesday. NATO shot down a second Iranian ballistic missile in Turkish airspace over Gaziantep, fragments falling between a US airbase and a NATO radar installation. The first intercept was March 4. Two Iranian missiles in NATO territory in six days, and Ankara has not invoked Article 5, though the question of how many more it absorbs before doing so is no longer theoretical.

The war has now killed more than 1,255 people in Iran and over 486 in Lebanon, according to health officials in both countries. Eleven Israelis and eight Americans are dead. Iran's parliament speaker pledged a harsh response to strikes on residential areas. The G7 energy ministers met Tuesday and again stopped short of releasing strategic oil reserves, saying only that they "stand ready." No ceasefire framework exists. No mediator has standing with both sides. And the country that says the war is almost over just flew more bombers over Iran than on any previous day.
