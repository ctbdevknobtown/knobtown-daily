Russia is feeding Iran intelligence on American forces. The war enters week two.

Russia is providing Iran with real-time intelligence on the locations and movements of American troops, ships, and aircraft, according to multiple US officials. It is the first indication that Moscow has sought to directly involve itself in the conflict. The intelligence sharing reportedly includes satellite imagery and signals intercepts. The White House has not publicly responded. The disclosure raises the stakes considerably: a nuclear-armed state is now actively supporting the other side of a war the United States is fighting.

The word on Wall Street is stagflation: rising energy prices on top of a cooling labor market, with the central bank stuck in the middle.
